    • We regularly review this Privacy Policy.
    • Data transfers. We use servers around the world and your information may be processed on servers located outside of the country where you live. Regardless of where your information is processed, we apply the same protections. We also comply with certain legal frameworks relating to the transfer of data.
    • Complaints. When we receive formal written complaints, we respond by contacting the person who made the complaint and by working with the regulatory authorities to resolve any complaints.
    • U.S. state law requirements. Some U.S. state privacy laws like the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) and Virginia Consumer Data Protection Act (VCDPA) require specific disclosures for state residents. Privacy Policy is designed to help you understand how MinuteZero handles your information:
      • We explain the categories of information MinuteZero collects and the sources of that information MinuteZero collects.
      • We explain how MinuteZero uses information in Why MinuteZero collects data.
      • We explain when MinuteZero may disclose information in Sharing your information.
      • MinuteZero does not sell your personal information. MinuteZero also does not “share” your personal information as that term is defined in the CCPA.
      • We explain how MinuteZero retains information.
    • State laws like the CCPA and VCDPA also provide the right to request information about how MinuteZero collects, uses, and discloses your information. And they give you the right to access and correct your information, and to request that MinuteZero delete that information. The VCDPA also provides the right to opt out of certain forms of profiling and targeted advertising. Finally, the CCPA and VCDPA provide the right to not be discriminated against for exercising these privacy rights. We describe the choices you have to manage your privacy and data across MinuteZero's services and to access, review, update and delete your information, as well as export and download a copy of it. If you have questions or requests related to your rights under the CCPA or VCDPA, you (or your authorized agent) can also contact MinuteZero. And if you disagree with the decision on your request, you can ask MinuteZero to reconsider it.
    • Your rights under the CCPA/CPRA. The CCPA/CPRA provides California residents with specific rights regarding their personal information. If you are a resident of California, you have the following rights as described in Privacy Policy:
      • The right to notice.
      • The right to know/access.
      • The right to say no to the sale or sharing of Personal Data (opt-out). We do not sell your Personal Data.
      • The right to correct Personal Data.
      • The right to limit use and disclosure of sensitive Personal Data.
      • The right to delete Personal Data.
      • The right not to be discriminated against.
    • Exercising your CCPA/CPRA Data Protection Rights. In order to exercise any of your rights under the CCPA/CPRA, and if you are a California resident, you can contact Us. Only you, or a person registered with the California Secretary of State that you authorize to act on your behalf, may make a verifiable request related to your personal information.
    • Your rights under the GDPR. You have the right under this Privacy Policy, and by law if you are within the EU, to:
      • Request access to your Personal Data.
      • Request correction of the Personal Data that We hold about you.
      • Object to processing of your Personal Data.
      • Request erasure of your Personal Data.
      • Request the transfer of your Personal Data.
      • Withdraw your consent.
    • You may use the controls available on MinuteZero.ai/terms, or you may exercise your rights of access, rectification, cancellation and opposition by contacting Us by sending an email to privacy@pairpr.com. Please note that we may ask you to verify your identity before responding to such requests.

Under California Civil Code Section 1798 (California's Shine the Light law), California residents with an established business relationship with us can request information once a year about sharing their Personal Data with third parties for the third parties' direct marketing purposes. California Privacy Rights for Minor Users (California Business and Professions Code Section 22581).
